Life After Secondary School:

Education Choices of Students in Australia

The transition from secondary school students marks a crucial moment in the lives of Australian students. Critical desicions regarding their future educational and career paths are being made. Undestanding the trends that influence their choices will provide valueble insights into the broader educational landscape. To better understand these dynamics, several visualisations are created to explore the number of tertiary education students in Australia.

The choropleth map above illutrates the distribution of university students per 10000 capita across various states in Australia. Each state is shaded based on this ratio, allowing for a comparison of student density relative to the population size. As the map suggests, despite having only a small area of size, Australian Capital Territory exhibits a high number of university students per 10000 capita, at 1368.88 unviersity students per 10000 capita. This suggests that Australian Capital Territory is home to prominent institutions like the Australian National University (ANU) and the University of Canberra, which attract students from across the country and internationally. On the other hand, Tasmania has only 928.54 unviersity students per 10000 capita. The reason behind that might be there are fewer higher education institutions compared to other states. The University of Tasmania (UTAS) is the main university in the state, and while it serves the local population, Tasmania doesn't attract as many interstate or international students.

The grouped bar chart on the left shows the number of students in public and private universities in each state, providing a clear comparison of the distribution of higher education institutions across Australia. We can clearly see that the number of students in public universities are more than the number of private institutions, showing the effort of the government in providing tertiary education for the people, making them more accesible across the country. As th results suggests we can observe that states such as New South Wales and Victoria both have more stuents in public universties, at 114,625 and 113,053 respectively, whereas the number of students in private institutions are way lesser. Interestingly, for states such as Northen Territory and Tasmania do not have any private institutions provided for students, showing their lack of options when choosing to continue their studies after secondary school.

The donut chart showed above provides information of the various level of study of university students in Australia in 2023. Each segment of the chart corresponds to a specific level. The 3 highlighted segments represents the 3 level of study with the highest number of students, which are Bachelor Degree, Postgraudate Degree and Certificate III/IV. For Bachelor Degree, it has 815,700 students currently studying, the most out of all levels. Then, the number of students studying in Certificate III/IV is at 510,000, which is also a high amount of students compared to others. The level with the third highest number of students is the Postgraudate Degree, at 311,500 students. This suggests that there are also a lot of students who continued to pursue their studies after their Bachelor Degrees, increasing their knowledge in respective fields.

The line chart above displayed the proportion of university students' main field of study from year 2013 to 2023. As of 2023, the main field of study that is most popular amongst students is engineering and related technologies, where approxiamtely 21.2% of students studying in that field. Besides that, information technologies have been slowly getting more popular over the years, rising from 5.1% in 2013 to 10.3% in 2023. This shows the trend of information technologies being the hot topic over the past few years. On the other hand, the number of students studying management and commerce have been slowly decreasing over the years, reaching only 15.9% in 2023, which suggests that this field might be slowly eliminated.

Field of apprenticeship

The radial bar chart on the left showed the proportion of field of trade of apprenticeship for university students in 2023. This provides information of the interest of university students in future working industry. From the chart, we can observe that the construction field shows a large proportion at 25.0%, showing the number of students that are currently heading in that direction. Automative engineering is also a popular field, contributing around 21.9%, showing that students with related skillsets have lots of oppoturnity in the workplace.
